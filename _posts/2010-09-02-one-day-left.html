If you are wondering, am I excited?, duh! Anyways this post won't be long just giving a quick run-down of my setup and the things that I am taking. When considering that I am going to be gone for about four months, I had to really consider the best way to keep things light. I decided that I wanted to be able to carry everything with me at one time, so that moving around would be manageable. To this end I decided on the following set-up:
  • One large backpack - 70L Osprey Aether: This is the main bag that holds the majority of things like my clothes, sleeping bag, camping equipment, etc.
  • One small backpack - ~20L Camelback with 3L bladder: This will be an everyday backpack I can take with me when sightseeing or doing day trips.
  • One messenger bag - Performance bike messenger bag: This is my main bag for carrying things like my computers and camera.
The smartest thing that I did was to use lot of smaller bags and stuff sacks within the other bags. This helps to compartmentalize things and keep things organized. I've put all computer things in a bag, socks in a bag, camping and cook equipment in a bag. The idea is that I want to be able to get to things quickly and keep them from getting disorganized.
